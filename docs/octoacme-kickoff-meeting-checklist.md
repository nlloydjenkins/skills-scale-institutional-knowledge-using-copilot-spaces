# OctoAcme — Project Kickoff Meeting Checklist

## Purpose
Ensure every new project or initiative launches with a shared understanding of goals, roles, scope, and process. Use this checklist to prepare for, run, and follow up on the kickoff meeting.

## When to use
Run the kickoff meeting after the project has been approved during initiation (see [Project Initiation](octoacme-project-initiation.md)) and before sprint planning begins (see [Project Planning](octoacme-project-planning.md)).

---

## Before the Meeting

- [ ] Project One-pager reviewed and approved by Product Lead
- [ ] Agenda shared with all attendees at least 24 hours in advance
- [ ] All key roles confirmed and invited (see [Roles & Personas](octoacme-roles-and-personas.md)):
  - [ ] Product Manager
  - [ ] Project Manager / Scrum Master
  - [ ] Engineering Manager or Tech Lead
  - [ ] Developer representatives
  - [ ] UX Designer (if applicable)
  - [ ] Business Analyst (if applicable)
  - [ ] Technical Writer (if applicable)
  - [ ] SRE / DevOps Engineer (if applicable)
  - [ ] Key stakeholders
- [ ] Project board / repo created and accessible
- [ ] Draft backlog or feature list available for review

---

## During the Meeting

### 1. Project Overview (5–10 min)
- [ ] Project Manager / Product Manager presents the problem statement and objectives
- [ ] Success metrics and Definition of Done reviewed
- [ ] Timeline and key milestones shared

### 2. Roles & Responsibilities (5 min)
- [ ] Each team member introduced with their role
- [ ] Decision-making authority (RACI) clarified
- [ ] Escalation path confirmed (team → PM → Product Lead → Sponsor)

### 3. Scope & Backlog Review (10–15 min)
- [ ] High-level scope reviewed and boundaries agreed
- [ ] Initial backlog walked through; major items discussed
- [ ] Out-of-scope items explicitly noted

### 4. Risks & Dependencies (5–10 min)
- [ ] Initial risks identified and owners assigned
- [ ] Known dependencies (internal and external) listed
- [ ] Mitigation or contingency options noted (see [Risks & Communication](octoacme-risks-and-communication.md))

### 5. Process & Ways of Working (5 min)
- [ ] Sprint cadence and ceremony schedule confirmed
- [ ] Communication channels and status reporting cadence agreed
- [ ] PR and branching conventions reviewed (see [Execution & Tracking](octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md))
- [ ] Documentation and release note ownership agreed (see [Release & Deployment](octoacme-release-and-deployment.md))

### 6. Questions & Actions (5 min)
- [ ] Open questions captured in the project board or shared doc
- [ ] Action items assigned with owners and due dates

---

## After the Meeting

- [ ] Meeting notes published and shared within 24 hours
- [ ] Action items tracked in project board
- [ ] Risk register initialized (see [Risks & Communication](octoacme-risks-and-communication.md))
- [ ] Project board updated with initial backlog items
- [ ] Kickoff summary sent to all stakeholders who could not attend
- [ ] Move to sprint planning when backlog and DoD are ready (see [Project Planning](octoacme-project-planning.md))

---

## Tips
- Keep the meeting to 60 minutes or less; schedule follow-up deep-dives for complex topics.
- Assign a note-taker before the meeting starts.
- Revisit this checklist in the first retrospective to identify any kickoff gaps.
